# Models Have a Shelf Life Now

A line popped up in Claude Code the other day:

> Fable 5 is here! Our newest model for complex, long-running work. Included in your plan limits until Jun 22, then switch to usage credits to continue.

A new model, built for complex, long-running work; on your plan until June 22, then pay-as-you-go after that.

The phrase "complex, long-running work" lit me up — that's all I do. So I treated it like treasure.

## How hard I went

I'm on the biggest plan there is. In a normal week I barely cross fifty-something percent of my limit. Never had to think about it.

Not this time. I had six sessions running side by side, each with multi-agent workflows inside it, and I fed it the heaviest, longest, most token-hungry work I had. All day, no stopping.

Then I watched the "7d" weekly-usage number on my status bar climb:

- Day three, evening: 88%.
- A few hours later: 94%.
- Shortly after: empty.

A quota I usually can't even half-spend in a week — gone in just over three days.

## Then it left early

The label said June 22. My plan was simple: even if I burned through the plan quota, I could keep using it until the 22nd, and pay per use after that. Either way, more time with it.

It didn't make it to the 22nd.

Not "out of quota" gone — the model itself went offline, and the API wouldn't reach it either. The little "I'll use it later" I'd been saving up hit nothing.

A model I'd spent three or four days with, just getting a feel for — gone, earlier than the date it printed itself. And it's not coming back.

## Models have a shelf life now

I used to assume tools are permanent. You install something and it's just there — works today, works tomorrow.

But a model isn't software. A model is more like fresh produce — it has a shelf life, and the date on the label is the optimistic estimate.

Once that clicked, I stopped regretting those three days of going all-in.

Had I paced myself, rationed the quota, told myself "it's fine, it lasts till the 22nd" — I'd be holding nothing now. No quota saved (it resets to zero), no extra work shipped (the model's gone).

The only quota you truly own is the part you spent today.

When something is this good and this finite, the only waste is hesitating.
